Frequently Asked Questions


How can I find a bike route that fits my capabilities?

All bike routes include information such as total distance, if it is kids friendly as well as a map of the route. Visitors can search through the available routes to find the one that meets their criteria.

Where are inforamtion about the cities and locations in my route are coming from?

All cities and sites information in the page are extracted from Wikipedia by webscraping.This site is utilizing Open Layer to create the map widget on the site. Open Layer is an open source mapping service, alternative to Google Maps.

How do I select a route?

Begin by selecting between a seaside route or a mountain path. After you have made your selection, you will be presented with a list of recommended routes indicating the starting city of your path and your ending destination. The first city is your starting point and the second you finish. Once you have made your selection, you will be presented with a list of infomation about the cities you are visiting, a map, the time it will take you to complete your path by bike and your total distance.

How is the estimated duration of a path calculated?

The estimated duration of a path is calculated based on the total distance between the two cities divided by the average speed of an amature biker, which is 25km/h or 15mph.

How do I start a new path search?

At any point you can click on the Bike Routes icon in the top left corner of your screen and you will return at the home page.
